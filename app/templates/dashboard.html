ⓘ How It Works — what every score and metric means, and how they're calculated
▼
THE THREE SCORES (0–100)
Golden Zone Score
The main score. Geometric mean of Buy × Exit — both sides need to be good. A county scoring 90 on buying but 10 on selling still gets a low Golden score. Formula: √(Buy Score × Exit Score)
Buy Score
How easy it is to acquire properties below market from motivated sellers. Driven by: Price Reduction % (primary — how many sellers are already cutting price), YoY price trend (falling = more desperation), and stale inventory ratio (active ÷ new listings — high = listings sitting unsold).
Exit Speed
How fast you can resell to a retail family buyer. Driven by: DOM (primary — lower is faster), Pending Ratio (more buyers under contract = strong demand), and Sale-to-List ratio (above 100% = bidding wars, below 97% = slow market).
KEY METRICS EXPLAINED
DOM (Days on Market)
Median days a listing sits before going under contract. <30 = fast (green). 30–60 = moderate. >60 = slow (red). Source: Realtor.com Research.
Price Drops %
% of active listings where the seller has cut the asking price at least once. 25%+ = meaningful distress signal. Source: Realtor.com Research.
Pending Ratio
Pending listings ÷ total active listings. 30%+ = strong buyer demand. 10%- = few buyers. Source: Realtor.com Research.
YoY %
Year-over-year change in median listing price. Negative = prices falling = buy opportunity. Sources: Realtor.com · Zillow ZHVI.
Sale-to-List
Actual sale price ÷ list price. >100% = sold above asking (competitive). <97% = buyer has leverage. Source: Redfin Data Center.
Market Heat
Zillow Market Heat Index — 0 (strong buyer) to 100 (strong seller). Direct read on where selling is easy. Source: Zillow Research.
Affordability
Median income ÷ median home value. Higher = more buyers can afford the market = easier to sell. Sources: Census ACS · Zillow ZHVI.
MAP & SCATTER GUIDE
County Map
Click a state to zoom into its counties. Click a county to see full detail. Layer buttons switch what the colors represent. Brighter = higher value for that metric.
Opportunity Matrix
X axis = how fast you can sell (right = faster). Y axis = how easy to buy cheap (up = more motivated sellers). Bubble size = market volume (active listings). Color = Golden score (green = good, red = avoid).
The Four Quadrants
Top-right Golden Zone — motivated sellers + fast resale. Target these.
Top-left Motivated sellers but slow to resell. Requires longer hold time.
Bottom-right Fast market but hard to buy cheap — prices aren't dropping yet.
Bottom-left Both slow and expensive to buy. Avoid.
Hunt Mode
A visual filter that strips the map down to just the counties worth looking at on the currently selected layer. Toggle it with the Hunt Mode button.
Top 3% — cyan ring + animated radar pulse. Your best targets on this layer.
Top 10% — white outline. Strong secondary options.
Middle 65% — normal color, 65% opacity.
Bottom 25% — heavily dimmed so they fade into the background.
Percentile direction is layer-aware: for layers where high is good (Golden, Buy, Exit, Income) the top performers pulse; for layers where low is good (DOM), the lowest values pulse. Hunt Mode works on both the national view and inside a drilled-down state.
